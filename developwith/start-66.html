mydigitalspace > mydigitalstructure
29 Nov 2011
mark.byers

Hi all,

To better represent and communicate the ibCom service and reflect what has been our role over the last 11 years, we have started the process of transitioning from the name "mydigitalspace" to "mydigitalstructure".

In the sense that ibCom provides the digital structures of which our partners use to create digital organisational information spaces.

In the same way that builders use steel, wood & concrete structures to build human living spaces.

In is only s slight tweak and wont have any really impact on existing services - other than the name and focusing the service around the mydigitalstructure.com domain going forward.   The mydigitalspace domains will all remain active well into the future.

Visual Data-Driven Documents (d3.js)
16 Nov 2011
mark.byers

This tool is compatible with mydigitalspace onDemand and allows for the presentation of infomation in different visual forms, within a web-browser.

You can see an example at http://mbostock.github.com/d3/tutorial/circle.html and more information at http://mbostock.github.com/d3/


This tool is used by application interface developers as a resource to create the visual views based on the information held within mydigitalspace onDemand.

Another great example of the power of an open interface layer.

mydsondemand.com - 300 pages!
28 Oct 2011
mark.byers

We just cracked the 300th web page on the http://mydsondemand.com website! - including getting started guides and all the details of the 280 methods.

The platform just keeps on getting more and more powerful - if you can imagine an organisational app - the mydigitalspace onDemand platform can probably turn it into a reality quicker than anything before it...

A Friday afternoon quote from someone we meet reccently - they spent 10 minutes trying to force mydigitalspace onDemand into the traditional standard software space and it failed - they then stopped, thought for 2 minutes and came out with:

"Put a line through mass produced user interfaces – they no longer need to exist because we now have information utilities based on a service oriented architecture - and organisations never wanted them.

Every use-case can be achieved based on the inherent economics contained within this much sort after architecture.  The "mass production" has shifted to the utility layer and no longer needs to live in the user experience layer.

Re-imagine everything..."

Very cool.
